Responsible Gaming

Last updated: 16-Aug-2024

Khiladi Zone, the operator of this online gaming website, https://www.khiladizone.com/ (“Website”, including the mobile application), is committed to promoting responsible gaming and preventing excessive gaming. This Responsible Gaming Policy outlines the Company’s commitments and your responsibilities to encourage responsible gaming practices and reduce the negative impacts of excessive gaming. Games may involve financial risk and can be addictive. Please play responsibly and at your own risk.

If you choose to play on our Website, here are some general guidelines to make your playing experience safer and help avoid excessive gaming: Add money to the Khiladi Zone Wallet only with funds you can afford to spend. Never add money to the Khiladi Zone Wallet with funds needed for essential items such as food, rent, bills, or tuition. Plan your purchases and the time you will spend playing on the Website in advance, and stick to that limit, regardless of your performance. Avoid playing when you are upset, tired, or depressed, as it is difficult to make good decisions when feeling down. Do not neglect personal and other important tasks in order to play. Complete all essential tasks so you can play with a clear and calm mind. Balance your gameplay duration with other activities. Engage in other forms of entertainment to ensure gaming does not become too dominant in your life. Do not borrow money to make purchases on the Website. Avoid using all the funds in the Khiladi Zone Wallet in a single game or session. Take regular time-outs and breaks. Only participate in games that match your skill level and financial capabilities. Avoid being tempted to join high-stakes tables even if you are winning consistently.

How do you know if you are addicted to gaming?


Ask yourself the following questions:

  • Have you often played longer than you planned?
  • Have you often played until your last rupee was gone?
  • Do thoughts of playing cause you to lose sleep?
  • Have you used your income or savings to play while letting bills go unpaid?
  • Have you made repeated, unsuccessful attempts to stop playing?
  • Have you broken the law or considered breaking the law to finance your gaming?
  • Have you borrowed money to finance your gaming?
  • Have you felt depressed or suicidal because of your gaming losses?
  • Have you been remorseful after gaming?
  • Have you ever played to get money to meet your financial obligations?
If you or someone you know answers yes to any of the above questions, please consider seeking professional help or advice.
